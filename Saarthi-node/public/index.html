FAQ

Ask Saarthi anything

Quick answers to the questions a traveler usually asks before trusting a platform.

How does Saarthi decide which places I see first?
If location permission is available, Saarthi tries to prioritize destinations closer to you. Otherwise, it shows a curated compact set of featured places with live weather details.
Do I need an account to explore places?
No. You can browse destinations without logging in. An account is only needed when you want to contribute a new location or keep personalized access available.
Where does the live weather information come from?
Saarthi fetches weather information in real time for destinations and also for the user’s current location when GPS permission is granted in the browser.
Can users add new travel places to the platform?
Yes. Logged-in users can submit a destination with city, state, region, category, description, map link, season, and optional coordinates, and Saarthi stores that data in the backend database.
Is Saarthi only for famous destinations?
Not at all. The curated catalog starts with recognizable destinations, but the submission flow is meant to grow the platform with lesser-known spots as well.

About Saarthi

Built to make trip planning feel simpler

Saarthi brings destination discovery, live weather, location-aware suggestions, map-ready planning, and community-added places into one cleaner travel experience. Instead of jumping between multiple sites, travelers can browse, compare, and plan from a single screen.

What Saarthi solves

Travel information is usually scattered across maps, blogs, weather pages, and social posts. Saarthi organizes that experience into a focused platform for discovery and planning.

What users can do

Search destinations, see live weather, check place details, track routes with maps, sign in, and contribute new locations that expand the travel catalog over time.

Why it feels practical

The platform favors usable details over clutter: compact place tabs, state context, local weather visibility, and GPS-aware discovery that adapts to the user’s position.
